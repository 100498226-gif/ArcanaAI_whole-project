## Purpose

## Definition of Confidential Information

Confidential Information includes, but is not limited to:

- Product roadmaps, feature specifications, and development timelines
- Pricing models and commercial strategies
- Customer lists, contracts, and usage data
- Technical architecture documents and source code
- Financial projections and internal performance metrics
- Marketing strategies and go-to-market plans
- Any information marked "Confidential" or "Proprietary" at the time of disclosure

---

## Obligations of the Receiving Party

The Receiving Party agrees to:

1. **Not disclose** Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent from the Disclosing Party.
2. **Use** Confidential Information solely for the purpose described in this Agreement.
3. **Protect** Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect their own confidential information, and no less than reasonable care.
4. **Notify** the Disclosing Party immediately if any unauthorized disclosure is discovered.
5. **Return or destroy** all Confidential Information upon request or upon termination of this Agreement.

---

## Term

The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement remain in force for a period of **3 years** from the Effective Date (i.e., until January 15, 2028).

After this period, information that has become publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party is no longer subject to these obligations.

---

## Exclusions

The obligations above do not apply to information that:

- Was already in the public domain at the time of disclosure
- Was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure
- Was independently developed by the Receiving Party
- Is required to be disclosed by law or court order (with prior notice to the Disclosing Party)
